# Horror-Series Production — Case Study Overview

## What This Is

An anonymised summary of a three-book cosmic horror-comedy series produced using the pipeline preserved in this repository. These case studies prove the system works and show what failure looks like.

## The Series (Anonymised)

A working-class cosmic horror series set in regional Australia. The protagonist is a pest control technician who discovers that the universe is full of interdimensional parasites and nobody is coming to save him.

**Three books produced:**
- Book 1: ~45,000 words, 17 chapters
- Book 2: ~44,613 words, 17 chapters
- Book 3: ~40,571 words, 17 chapters

**Production timeline:** Book 1 (naive), Book 2 (discipline), Book 3 (maturity)

## Key Failures by Book

### Book 1
- No architecture document → wandering narrative
- No tracking → didn't know what was missing
- No handoff → "where did we leave off?" every session
- Editorial too gentle → structural gaps missed
- No Line/Copy Edit → prose issues accumulated
- Middle act sag (chapters 6–9)

### Book 2
- Chapter architecture conflation (Chapter 4 stole beats from 5-6)
- Word count shrinkage invisible at draft stage
- Scope creep: multiple frontiers considered simultaneously
- Editorial scope creep during Line Edit
- Reader contamination (saw editorial reports)

### Book 3
- First draft shrinkage: 33,131 words (vs. 42,000 for Book 2)
- Seductive entity harder to maintain than aggressive entities
- Thread continuity gaps (frequency mentions dropped in Ch 11)
- Cadence fatigue (chapters 14-17 ended same way)
- Reader Audit found Marco's sacrifice slightly rushed

## Key Successes by Book

### Book 1
- Completed manuscript proved the concept
- Reader Audit identified genuine engagement
- Humor-horror rhythm established
- Working-class voice proved sustainable

### Book 2
- Autonomy grant tripled drafting velocity
- Multi-profile separation improved quality
- Discovery Pass prevented editorial assumptions
- Scope discipline kept focus
- Tier-Based Expansion prioritised effectively

### Book 3
- First draft completed in single session (17 chapters, 33,131 words)
- Seductive entity (beauty-as-trap) expanded the series' emotional range
- Reader Audit classification framework refined prioritisation
- Verification Rule caught every instance of report drift
- Thread-continuity gap detection added to workflow
- Cadence fatigue detection improved ending variety

## What the Next Project Should Do Differently

1. **Deploy the full 9-stage system from Book 1** — don't discover stages in production
2. **Track word counts from draft 1** — flag shrinkage immediately
3. **Use multi-profile from the start** — Writer/Editor/Reader separation improves quality
4. **Add Line Edit and Copy Edit to initial pipeline** — not as afterthoughts
5. **Make series-length decision explicit in architecture** — 70k? 45k? Vary?
6. **Run Reader Audit after Pass 3.5, before Line Edit** — not after assembly
7. **Document the "why did this stop being frightening?" question** — part of every concept brief
