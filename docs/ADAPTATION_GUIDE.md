# Adaptation Guide

How to customise the novel production system for different genres, styles, and project scales.

## Genre-Specific Adaptations

### Cosmic / Biological Horror (The Original)

The system was built for this. Use it as-is.

**Key adaptations already present:**
- Entity escalation (different horror mechanic per book)
- Rotating backpacker / opening lens
- Humor-horror rhythm discipline
- Working-class pragmatism as voice anchor

### Gothic Horror

**What to add:**
- Atmosphere ledger: track weather, season, time of day per scene
- Architecture as character: building descriptions accumulate damage/decay
- Inheritance / family secret tracking
- POV: first-person retrospective ("I did not know then that...") or limited third with gothic distance

**What to modify:**
- The "lean chapter" rule: gothic thrives on slow accumulation; lean chapters may be intentional
- Line Edit: pay special attention to sentence length variation — gothic needs long, then short

### Psychological Horror

**What to add:**
- Unreliable narrator tracking: what does the reader know vs. what the narrator claims
- Reality ledger: which scenes are "real" vs. hallucination/dream/unreliable perception
- Character psychology progression: document the narrator's mental state chapter by chapter

**What to modify:**
- Discovery Pass must include a "reality audit" — which events are confirmed by other characters?
- Reader Audit question: "At what point did you stop trusting the narrator?" (this is desired; the question is WHEN, not WHETHER)

### Folk Horror

**What to add:**
- Folklore ledger: which customs, songs, rituals appear, and their real-world sources
- Community dynamics: who believes, who performs, who resists
- Seasonal/calendar tracking: folk horror is often tied to specific dates

**What to modify:**
- Architecture: the "ordinary world" is the folk world; the horror is the intrusion of modernity OR the revelation that the folk world was always hostile
- Expansion rule: folk horror needs space for ritual and custom; chapters may need to be longer for atmosphere

### Survival Horror

**What to add:**
- Resource tracker: food, ammunition, equipment condition, group morale
- Threat proximity map: where is the threat now vs. where was it
- Decision ledger: every choice the group makes and its consequence

**What to modify:**
- Pacing: shorter chapters, higher chapter count. Survival horror runs on escalation and exhaustion.
- The "heartbeat moment" rule: survival horror may need them more frequently (every 2-3 chapters)

### Mystery / Detective (Cozy or Hardboiled)

**What to add:**
- Clue tracker: every clue planted, its chapter, its payoff chapter
- Red herring ledger: intentional misdirections
- Timeline: day-by-day with specific times

**What to modify:**
- Editorial stage: developmental editorial MUST check clue-plant/payoff balance
- Reader Audit: "Did you solve it before the reveal?" (ideal answer: "I suspected but wasn't sure")

## Scale Adaptations

### Standalone Novel (Not a Series)

- Remove series-specific tracking (SERIES_BIBLE, cross-book continuity)
- Remove rotating-backpacker pattern
- Keep the 9-stage pipeline; it works for standalone
- Add a "theme seeding" checklist: ensure the central theme appears in chapters 1, middle, and finale

### Short Series (2-3 Books)

- Keep series bible but lighter: character arcs only, no long-term escalation table
- Multi-profile pipeline: master + writer + editor + reader is still valuable
- Handoff system: essential for maintaining continuity across 2-3 books

### Long Series (5+ Books)

- Add a SERIES_EXPANSION_STRATEGY.md from the start
- Use the scope discipline rules (single-frontier per book)
- Add a SEED_LEDGER.md: every hook planted and its target book
- Consider adding a RESEARCHER profile for maintaining factual consistency

## Style Adaptations

### First-Person Present Tense

- The original system was built for this. Use as-is.
- Line Edit: pay attention to "I think about this" throat-clearing
- Voice consistency: the narrator's voice is the series' engine; protect it fiercely

### Third-Person Limited (Rotating POV)

- Add a POV tracker: which chapters use which POV character
- Voice consistency becomes harder: each POV needs distinct diction and rhythm
- Editorial stage: add a "POV voice audit" — do the different POVs feel different?

### Third-Person Omniscient

- Remove first-person-specific rules (throat-clearing, "I" filtering)
- Add a "narrative distance" tracker: how close is the narrator to each scene
- Editorial stage: check for head-hopping within scenes

## Project-Specific Rules That Always Apply

Regardless of genre or scale:

1. **The manuscript is the ground truth.**
2. **Never trust a report without verification.**
3. **Handoff before report.**
4. **Discovery before editorial.**
5. **Copy edit is mechanical only.**
6. **Autonomy triples velocity.**
7. **Every book teaches the pipeline.**

These seven rules are non-negotiable. They are the reason the system works.
