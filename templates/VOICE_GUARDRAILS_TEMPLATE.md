## Operational Rules

1. **Regional markers are light by default.** Stronger dialect may be used sparingly when it performs clear dramatic purpose: changing power, belonging, threat, intimacy, withholding, or social exclusion.
2. **Class texture comes through life, money, work, housing, weather — not vocabulary.**
3. **Avoid stage-[region] caricature.** Prohibited phrases: [LIST]
4. **Dialect is not decoration.** If plain English says it better, use plain English.
5. **Unfamiliar terms must be self-explanatory from context.** No glossaries. No footnotes.
6. **Profanity is punctuation, not emphasis.**
7. **When uncertain, prefer plain grounded English over exaggerated dialect.**
8. **Voice must survive the cosmic scale.** If the series escalates to existential horror, the voice must remain intimate.

---

## Dramatic-Purpose Dialect Rule

Stronger dialect may be used when it changes what the reader understands about:
- Power
- Threat
- Intimacy
- Exclusion
- Belonging
- Withholding

**Test:** If removing the dialect would not change any of those dimensions, use plain English instead.

## The Confidence Test

Before finalising any sentence, ask:

1. Does this sound like something [protagonist] would actually think?
2. Would [protagonist] use this word in this situation?
3. Does this sentence advance the reader's understanding of power, threat, or intimacy?
4. Would a reader from [region] recognise this as authentic, not performative?
5. Does this sentence survive removal from context? (If not, it may be too dependent on setup.)
6. If I remove the dialect from this sentence, does anything meaningful change? (If not, use plain English.)
